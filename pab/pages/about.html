About Us

Vision

We as parents face different challenges in our parenting role. These challenges are usually greater if we are parenting our children in a foreign country. International parents need to navigate their parenting experience in a foreign context with potentially less social support and guidance. In addition, they need to find the balance between the norms and expectations of their origin culture and that of the culture they live in. Despite these extra challenges, there is little support to enhance their well-being and experiences.

Parenting across Borders envisions a world where international parents experience sufficient support from their surroundings and feel confident in their role as parents.

Wij als ouders staan voor verschillende uitdagingen in onze ouderschapsrol. Deze uitdagingen zijn meestal groter als we onze kinderen in het buitenland opvoeden. Internationale ouders moeten hun rol als nieuwe ouder ontdekken in een vreemd land, met minder (tot geen) steun uit hun sociale omgeving. Bovendien voelen ze zich vaak gevangen tussen de normen en verwachtingen van hun eigen cultuur en die van het land waarin ze leven. Ondanks deze uitdagingen is er weinig steun beschikbaar om deze ouders te ondersteunen en hen te helpen hun welzijn te vergroten.

Parenting across Borders streeft naar een wereld waarin internationale ouders voldoende steun ervaren van hun omgeving en zich zelfverzekerd voelen in hun rol als ouder.

Mission

Parenting across borders is a social initiative which provides individual support for international parents in a dialogue group setting. Through dialogue we aim at providing international parents a safe setting where we allow a process of self-reflection, personal learning and a sense of belonging to a community. Moreover, by meeting other parents and hearing their experiences, parents receive validation and recognition of the different challenges they face as part of their role when parenting a child in a foreign context.

We believe that supported parents are confident parents who relate better to their inner-self, their children and their surroundings.

Parenting across Borders is een maatschappelijk initiatief dat individuele ondersteuning biedt aan internationale ouders in een dialooggroep. Door middel van dialoog willen wij internationale ouders een veilig ruimte bieden waarin we ruimte scheppen voor we een proces van zelfreflectie en persoonlijk leren. Tevens is de dialooggroep een plek waar de nieuwe ouders het gevoel ervaren om bij een gemeenschap te horen. Door andere ouders te ontmoeten en naar hun ervaringen te luisteren, vinden de ouders bovendien herkenning en ook erkenning voor de verschillende uitdagingen waarmee ze worden geconfronteerd bij het opvoeden van een kind in een vreemde context.

Wij geloven dat ondersteunde ouders meer zelfverzekerde ouders zijn, die beter zorgen voor hun innerlijke zelf, hun kinderen en hun omgeving.

Values we follow

  • Respect

    We will respect the dignity and worth of every individual and we will promote and practice understanding. We will maintain the confidentiality as required. We will also show respect for all persons equally without distinction whatsoever of race, gender, religion, colour, national or ethnic origin, language, marital status, sexual orientation, age, socio-economic status, disability, political conviction, or any other distinguishing feature.

  • Hope

    We believe that hope is an essential driver / element in any transformation process.

  • Humility

    Every experience and perspective are respected and recognized. Our expertise lies in acknowledging the different experiences and personal knowledge that each one of us carries/behold and which the communities we work with brings to the dialogue.

  • Honesty and transparency

    We act with transparency and honesty within our team and with all of our beneficiaries.

  • Responsibility

    We act with responsibility toward the communities in which we work and for the benefit of the communities that we serve.

Our team and board members

  • Annemarie Sweeris

    Annemarie Sweeris has a background in human rights work and conflict transformation and then trained to become a mediator, trainer and team coach. She aims to enhance meaningful connections between people (in communities, teams) through open communication and dialogue. She has co-created two games, the Ken-je-buur Spel and the Women’s Sanctuary Circle, which are geared to do just that. Her work as a board member with PAB stems from the desire to create a space for new parents for finding their own voice and the support system they need to thrive in their life’s roles.

  • Özge Bilgili

    Özge Bilgili is an assistant professor of interdisciplinary social science within European Research Centre on Migration and Ethnic Relations at Utrecht University, the chair of the Dutch Association for Migration Research (DAMR) and a nominated member of Utrecht Young Academy. Her expertise is on immigrant integration, transnationalism and policy analysis in related areas. In collaboration with the founder of Parenting Across Borders, Najuan Daadleh-Terpstra, Özge Bilgili organised the Utrecht University funded public engagement project Mothering across Border Dialogue Series in Utrecht. She is now a board member of Stichting Parenting Across Borders to engage with international parents and where relevant, offer the contribution of academic research, knowledge and debates.

  • Najuan Daadleh-Terpstra

    Najuan Daadleh-Terpstra is a conflict resolution practitioner and a dialogue facilitator. She earned her master degree in Conflict Resolution from George Mason University. Over the last twenty years she facilitated and implemented diverse community projects in different countries while aiming at enhancing the well-being of the communities involved. Najuan`s professional interest and focus has slightly shifted after becoming a mother in a migration context. As a first-generation migrant she has recognized the need for establishing a community and support system around pregnancy and the transition to motherhood in foreign context. Based on this need and the need of other international parents she has met and worked with, she co-founded in collaboration with PaB board members, Parenting across Border`s program and activities.
